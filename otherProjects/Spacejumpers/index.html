A Spacejumpers
guide to the Galaxy

The Moon

Ground zero for spacejumping

Average gravity on the moon: 1.625 m/s2, about 16.6% of that on Earth
A must-jump location for every Spacejumper wanting to earn the title.

Mars

The red planet

Average gravity on Mars: 3.721 m/s2, about 38% of that on Earth
You can't be a Spacejumper without having jumped on Earths red little sibling.

Mercury

Closest planet to the sun

Average gravity on Mercury: 3.7 m/s2, about 38% of that on Earth
With its proximity to the sun and lack of atmosphere, Mercury is a thrill for even the most hardcore jumper.

Titan

Largest moon of Saturn

Average gravity on Titan: 1.352 m/s2, about 13.8% of that on Earth
Do you like ice? If so, Titan is what you're looking for with ice covering the whole planet.

Pluto

A dwarf planet in the Kuiper belt

Average gravity on Pluto: 0.620 m/s2, about 6.3% of that on Earth
With a gravity only 6.3% of that on earth landing will not be a problem, not flying of the planet will.

Europa

The smallest of the four Galilean moons orbiting Jupiter

Average gravity on Europa: 1.314 m/s2, about 13.4% of that on Earth
You haven't really jumped the Solar System if you haven't jumped one of Jupiters moons.
